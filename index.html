All-in-One Realistic Weather Editing. We introduce Cyclone, a diffusion-based framework for weather editing in autonomous driving. It can both synthesize high-fidelity diverse weather conditions (left) and remove adverse effects (right) from images and videos. In particular, this is achieved without requiring any ground-truth paired data during training.

Abstract

Robust perception under diverse weather conditions is a major challenge in autonomous driving systems. A common strategy is to improve robustness by synthesizing adverse weather for training perception models or by applying weather-removal techniques to recover clean inputs. Existing approaches either rely on physics-based, task-specific models or on data-augmentation methods that require paired training samples, and often fail to synthesize realistic weather effects or preserve scene structure in out-of-domain scenarios. We present Cyclone, a unified framework for weather editing based on latent diffusion, enhanced with cycle-consistent training and knowledge from image-text models. Cyclone enables the synthesis of multiple weather conditions across diverse scenes without requiring paired training data. Furthermore, the model is used to generate pseudo ground-truth data for paired sequences, enabling the fine-tuning of a video diffusion model for temporally consistent editing. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach produces more realistic, structure-preserving outputs than existing baselines and leads to consistent improvements across several downstream driving perception tasks.

Results

Cyclone is a task-agnostic, all in one weather (clear, fog, rainy, snowy, night) editing model.


From single images to videos

It can be extended to video inference by generating pseudo GT for paired sequence and fine-tuning of a SVD model for temporally consistent editing.


From single-view to multi-view

In driving datasets, cameras are typically positioned in a structured left-front-right arrangement, providing shared information across views. We simply stack the multi-view images without any view-specific design as the self-attention layers ensure that each viewpoint aggregates information from the others.


On both synthetic and real life

We train and evaluate on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating its ability to generalize across different domains.


On famous driving datasets

Cyclone can be applied to many driving datasets (e.g., KITTI, nuScenes, Pandaset, Waymo, etc.), allowing perception systems to be tested on more scenarios.


And cities across the world, regardless of day or night


We outperform SoTA unpair editing techiques

SoTA methods usually focus on object-centric editing and do not work percisely for weather in driving scenarios.


While being smaller, faster, and more efficient than general instruction-based editing models trained on paired data

Instruction-based editing models relied on hand-crafted paired data, which is infeasible to obtain in this case and often fails to capture the complexity of real-world weather conditions. Competitive results can be achieved with orders of magnitude lower resources: 20x smaller model size, 1000x less training data, and 100x faster inference speed.


Improve upon driving downstream tasks

Removing adverse weather effects can improve the performance of downstream perception tasks by providing clearer inputs to the models.

Method

Our image translation pipeline includes a forward translation from $x_t$ to $\bar{y}$, conditioned on the text prompt $c_y$ and input image $x_0$, followed by a backward translation from $y_t$ to $\bar{x}$, conditioned on $c_x$ and $\bar{y}$. We enforce this cycle consistency using $\mathcal{L}_{\text{recon}}$ and $\mathcal{L}_{\text{cycle}}$. To further stabilize training and prevent collapse to the trivial identity mapping, we introduce a teacher model $D_\phi$ that provides regularization. The teacher is initialized with the same weights as the student model and is subsequently updated using an EMA of the student parameters. Notably, we use the same student model $D_\theta$ across multiple transitions.
Cyclone quickly converges to a stable solution, while the baseline which has no regularization collapses to the trivial identity mapping, requiring early stopping.
Cyclone-V equipped with temporal attention not only maintains the editing quality of Cyclone-I but also improves consistency throughout the sequence.
